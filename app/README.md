# ShotLab Form Check — on-device PWA

## 🔴 Live app (HTTPS — camera + mic work here)
**https://jmakula87.github.io/shotlab/**  — open on your phone (Chrome), tap
**Add to Home Screen** to install. Auto-deploys from `app/` on every push to
`master` (GitHub Pages, `.github/workflows/pages.yml`).

At the court: set the camera **close, side-on, no rim needed**, tap **🔴 Live**,
allow the camera, and shoot. After each shot the app **speaks** a short cue in
your ears (headphones-in flow) — bend your knees more, hold your follow-through,
etc. All processing is on-device.

A phone web-app that runs **MediaPipe pose on your device** (no upload, no
server), detects each shot from your release motion, measures your form, and
compares it to your `profile.json` — instant feedback vs your own normal.

## What it does
- **Live mode:** continuous on-device pose + rolling buffer detects a shot from
  your release motion, analyzes it, and gives per-shot feedback — **spoken aloud**
  (toggle "🔊 speak feedback") plus an on-screen card.
- Or **pick a recorded clip** for the same analysis with the skeleton overlaid.
- Measures elbow-at-release, knee bend, tempo (dip→release), follow-through
  hold, and balance drift.
- Compares to `profile.json`'s **ideal** (your own norm) → deltas + cues. Cues
  correct toward *your* norm; for metrics with a clear better direction only the
  fixable side speaks (a good deviation stays quiet).
- Also carries a `textbook` block (universal targets — entry ~45°, flare ~0°),
  kept separate from your personal ideal.
- **Feel tags (optional):** say "good"/"off" after a shot to tag it (trains your
  profile); export all tags as CSV with the ⬇️ button. Not required for feedback.
- "Jump to release" freezes the release frame and overlays your profile's ideal
  **skeleton** (gold) on yours (green).

## Run it (test on your Pixel, same Wi-Fi as the PC)
From `Desktop/ShotLab`:
```
python -m http.server 8080 --directory app
```
On the Pixel's browser open:  `http://<PC-LAN-IP>:8080`   (e.g. http://192.168.4.52:8080)

- File-upload works over plain HTTP (what we use here).
- **Live camera** and full "Install to Home Screen" need HTTPS — that comes when
  we host it (e.g. GitHub Pages, free HTTPS). For now, record then pick the clip.

## Notes / limits (honest)
- **Pose only** — the ball/arc (release angle) is the heavy on-device model,
  deferred to a later app version. Elbow flare needs the 2-camera 3D rig.
- The `profile.json` is **real** — generated by `tools/export_profile.py` from
  your own good shots (ideal metrics + ideal skeletons). Re-export after new
  sessions to keep it current.
- On one close camera the in-plane metrics (elbow bend, knee, tempo, follow,
  balance) are reliable; depth-dependent form (flare) is not — that's the S8.
- Speech: recognition (feel tags) and synthesis (spoken feedback) are browser
  APIs — verify they work in Chrome on the phone. If pose misses you: film
  closer / brighter, whole body in frame.

## Files
- `index.html`, `styles.css` — shell/UI
- `js/pose.js` — MediaPipe on-device pose (GPU→CPU fallback)
- `js/analyze.js` — phases, angles, compare-to-profile
- `js/live.js` — live camera + shot detection from release motion
- `js/say.js` — spoken feedback (text-to-speech cues)
- `js/voice.js` — feel-tag speech recognition ("good"/"off")
- `js/feelcsv.js` — export feel tags as CSV
- `js/overlay.js` — skeleton + ideal-skeleton overlay
- `js/main.js` — wiring
- `profile.json` — your ideal (+ textbook targets + skeletons)
- `manifest.json`, `sw.js`, `icon.svg` — PWA install/offline
